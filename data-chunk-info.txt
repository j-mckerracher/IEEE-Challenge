IEEE Computer Society Global Student Challenge Data Chunk Info
Created: 2026-06-10

This file documents how the extracted FRESCO Parquet data was divided into the
three challenge chunks:

1. participant
   Released to competitors when the challenge starts.

2. validation
   Released to competitors one week before submissions are due.

3. judge
   Held back for final judging only. Competitors should never receive this set.


Split principle
---------------

The data was split temporally by month and cluster/file group. The goal was to
keep the public participant data earlier in time, the validation data later in
time, and the final judging data later still. This reduces leakage risk compared
with random file-level splitting.

The split also avoids very sparse or unusual months. Those files were left in:

fresco-data-6-9-26\unzipped-data

Validation chunk
----------------

Folder:

validation

Included data:

S-suffix files:
2017-01 through 2017-03

C-suffix files:
2017-01 through 2017-03

No-suffix files:
2023-03

The validation chunk is intentionally smaller than the participant chunk. That is
acceptable because validation does not need to contain as many rows as the initial
training/release data. It should still contain enough labeled jobs and enough
failure/timeout examples to make the validation score meaningful.

Notes
-----

The split was based on hourly Parquet file names and monthly coverage. For final
challenge packaging, the downstream train/validation/test examples should still
be constructed at the job level so that the same job cannot leak across chunks.

Observed outcome labels include:

COMPLETED
FAILED
TIMEOUT
CANCELLED
NODE_FAIL

The challenge wording currently focuses on predicting FAILED or TIMEOUT jobs.
Before final packaging, decide whether CANCELLED and NODE_FAIL should be excluded,
treated as separate non-success outcomes, or folded into the positive class.
